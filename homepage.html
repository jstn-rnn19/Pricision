Terms and Terminology list

Material Cost

total expense of all raw ingredients and supplies used to make a product.

Unit Cost.

total cost to produce one single unit of your product.

Batch Yield

total quantity or number of finished products you get from one complete production run or batch.

Profit Margin

the percentage of your selling price that’s pure profit after covering all costs.

Markup

amount you add to your product’s cost to set the selling price.

Selling Price

the final amount you charge customers for your product.

Step by Step

Step 1

1.1 From the homepage, click the button in the hero section saying Add your First Product.

1.2. You’ll arrive at the Add Product Homepage.

1.3. Choose either Continue to Add Simple Product Single COGS Entry or Continue to Add Recipe Product Ingredients Packaging.

1.4. If you pick Simple Product, you’ll go to the simple product form.

1.5. Fill in Product Name, Pricing Method, Desired Margin or Markup (%), and optionally Product Description.

1.6. Click Save Product.

Step 2

2.1 If no materials are saved yet, you’ll be taken to the Add Material page.

2.2. Fill out the form with:

  • Material Name
  • Supplier (optional)
  • Purchase Price (₱) per Pack
  • Shipping Fee (₱)
  • Net Weight/Quantity per Pack (Base Unit)
  • Base Unit Type (used in the recipe)

2.3. Click Save Ingredient/Material to add the ingredient or material.

2.4. Repeat adding materials until all needed ingredients or material are saved.

2.5. When done, click Recipe Builder.

2.6. You’ll be redirected to the product home page.

Step 3

3.1 Choose to add or edit a recipe.

3.2. You’ll enter the Recipe Builder.

3.3. Fill in the Recipe Batch Yield (final units).

3.4. If no ingredients are listed, click Add Ingredient/Material.

3.5. In the form, select the ingredient/material.

3.6. Enter the net weight or quantity needed for your product.

3.7. Review the cost summary.

3.8. Click Calculate & Save Recipe.
